This research explores the potential of using artificial intelligence to simulate large-scale public deliberation by creating a 'synthetic citizenry' composed of AI agents, each trained on different moral philosophical frameworks and cultural perspectives. The aim is to develop a novel approach to estimating public consensus on complex policy issues through structured multi-agent deliberation.

The project involves developing AI agents capable of reasoning about moral implications using various ethical frameworks (e.g., utilitarianism, deontology, virtue ethics, care ethics) and training them on extensive datasets of moral reasoning, philosophical texts, and real-world policy deliberations. These agents will engage in structured debates and voting processes on actual policy proposals, with their interactions designed to mirror genuine democratic deliberation.

The research will examine whether such synthetic deliberation can provide meaningful insights into potential societal consensus, while addressing key questions about representation, bias, and the validity of AI-generated moral reasoning. The project aims to develop both a theoretical framework for synthetic democratic deliberation and practical tools for policy analysis.
